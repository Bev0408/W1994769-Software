## Summary of Module Content

The module allows students to demonstrate in applied, written and oral forms the effective use of competences required to achieve the degree of study. It requires students to combine previously acquired knowledge and techniques, with new knowledge/ideas gained from investigation and research, and produce an extended piece of work related to their degree of study. It involves the conceptualisation, design, implementation and evaluation of a substantial piece of software, process, model or experimental study.

---

## Module Aims

The aims of the module are to provide the student with an understanding of the skills, methodology and processes to enable the student to implement a project starting from a 'natural language specification' through to successful project implementation, documentation, and deployment. On completion of the module, the student will be equipped to work autonomously on real-world challenges.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the module the successful student will be able to:

- **LO1** Select, justify and apply appropriate methods, techniques and tools for tackling a large problem.
- **LO2** Develop a project plan, organise own activities and manage time and workload within the given timescale.
- **LO3** Collect and analyse project requirements using appropriate tools and techniques.
- **LO4** Research and collate relevant information from various sources and critically evaluate the findings taking any commercial and economic context into account.
- **LO5** Work autonomously to learn new skills/enhance existing knowledge and produce deliverables agreed with the supervisor.
- **LO6** Identify and take into account any EDI, legal, social, ethical and professional issues related to the problem and its solution including sustainability considerations.
- **LO7** Produce an extended piece of practical work (software, process, model, experimental study).
- **LO8** Produce a coherent report that documents and critically evaluates the practical work, the new skills and the effectiveness of the project plan.
- **LO9** Demonstrate and defend their work at a viva voce examination.

## Teaching and Learning Methods

The T&L rationale of the FYP module is to support students develop into independent learners, apply the knowledge and skills gained from the degree they study, gain new knowledge and address the challenges imposed by the FYP. Students are allocated a supervisor that guides and directs them throughout the duration of the module. The supervisor allocation is carried out on the basis of matching students' preference and supervisors' expertise, interests and availability.

Students select a project topic based on their interest, and are also exposed to project topics proposed by academics, reflecting on-going research at the School of Computer Science and Engineering. The project is agreed between the student and the supervisor to ensure it complies with the FYP requirements. Students work independently, forming, executing and adapting a project plan depending on developing circumstances, in consultation with their supervisor.

Teaching and learning methods include independent learning, using material and guidance provided on Blackboard, scheduled workshops and regular supervisory meetings, and independent study.

## Assessment Rationale

The assessment covers both the product delivered by the student at the end of the module, and the process by which this product was developed. The module is assessed by:

### Project Proposal and Requirements Specification (PPRS) — 15%

A presentation (up to 20 minutes) of a project proposal that enables students to engage with a project idea. The proposal will require the student to carry out background research, aims and objectives, and produce a detailed statement of requirements **(LO2, LO3, LO4)**.

Content includes: aims, objectives and scope of the project; definition of the problem; initial literature review; requirements gathering process; a detailed list of requirements; time schedule; bibliography.

### Interim Progress Demonstration (IPD) — 15%

A presentation (up to 20 minutes) on the initial output from design and practical work (e.g. demonstration of a software prototype, development tools, research progress) that enables students to progress toward the development of their Final Year Project deliverables **(LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5)**.

Content includes: stakeholders; modelling requirements and relevant diagrams; listing the project requirements; proposed design of the software/system to be developed, or plan of study and tools to be used in case an experimental study or theoretical work is being conducted. The IPD also includes a practical demonstration of output capturing key functionality.

### Final Year Project (FYP) — 70%

Comprises a written report (up to 12,000 words excluding diagrams and/or code), practical work, and a viva **(LO1-LO9)**.

The FYP is assessed for:
- Logical structure, coherence and relevance.
- Clear documentation on the scope, depth and complexity of the work.
- Background research.
- Development of new and existing skills.
- Selection and application of appropriate techniques.
- The different aspects of the development process.
- Any legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues relevant to the work.
- Reflection and critical evaluation of the work.

The practical output is assessed in terms of level of complexity, stability, robustness, performance and originality.

Students are expected to attend a 1-hour viva assessed by two members of staff (normally the project supervisor and another member of staff).

## Project Assessment Threshold Criteria

### 90–100

LO1–LO9. The project is complete, with major critical additions by student. An exemplary piece of work carried out to an extremely high professional standard. Publishable project.

- **LO1:** Exemplary selection, justification and application of appropriate methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Exemplary project management and scheduling.
- **LO3:** Exemplary requirements elicitation; exceptionally clear analysis and specification.
- **LO4:** Exceptionally coherent background research with highly creative and original commentary.
- **LO5:** Very high technical difficulty. Exceptional development of existing and new skills.
- **LO6:** Exceptional understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues.
- **LO7:** Exceptionally well-presented design, comprehensive justification, exhaustive testing.
- **LO8:** Exceptionally well-written and well-organised professional report with clear conclusions.
- **LO9:** Very deep understanding demonstrated in the viva. Exceptional, novel, stable and robust software.

### 80–89

LO1–LO9. The project is complete, with major critical additions by student. An outstanding piece of work carried out to a high professional standard.

- **LO1:** Outstanding selection, justification and application of methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Outstanding project management and scheduling.
- **LO3:** Outstanding requirements elicitation; very clear analysis and specification.
- **LO4:** Outstanding coherently presented background research with excellent critical analysis.
- **LO5:** Technically difficult and challenging project. Excellent development of existing and new skills.
- **LO6:** Excellent understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues.
- **LO7:** Well-presented design, comprehensive justification, exhaustive testing.
- **LO8:** Excellent, well-written and well-organised report with clear conclusions.

### 70–79

LO1–LO9. The project is complete, with major critical additions by student. An excellent piece of work carried out to a very high standard.

- **LO1:** Excellent selection, justification and application of methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Excellent project management and scheduling.
- **LO3:** Excellent requirements elicitation; very clear analysis and specification.
- **LO4:** Excellently presented background research with outstanding critical analysis.
- **LO5:** High technical difficulty. Significant development of existing and new skills.
- **LO6:** Excellent understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues.

- **LO3:** Very good requirements elicitation; good clear analysis and specification, though not always sufficiently detailed.

- **LO6:** Good understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues.

- **LO8:** Well-written and well-organised report; conclusions not clearly structured.
- **LO9:** Understanding of practical material demonstrated but not all theoretical aspects. Software has almost all required features, good performance and is mostly stable and robust.

### 50–59

LO1–LO9. The project is complete, with some work above 'textbook' standard. A good piece of work carried out to a good standard.

- **LO1:** Good selection, justification and application of methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Good project management and scheduling.
- **LO3:** Good requirements elicitation; analysis and specification not sufficiently well described but a relatively clear picture is present.
- **LO4:** Background research presented well; good critical analysis demonstrated, though at times lacking coherence.
- **LO5:** Technically sound and fairly challenging project. Good development of existing skills; some development of new skills. Has needed some supervisor help.
- **LO6:** Fair understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues.
- **LO7:** Design adequately presented but too brief. Justifications not convincing. Almost adequate testing with some minor gaps.
- **LO8:** Adequately written report; conclusions brief and at times not informative.
- **LO9:** Understanding of practical material demonstrated but not theoretical aspects. Software has acceptable features and performance, reasonably stable.

### 40–49

LO1–LO9. The project is complete, but nothing much above 'textbook'. Overall a fair piece of work carried out to a minimal standard but sufficient to pass without further work.

- **LO1:** Fair selection, justification and application of methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Fair project management and scheduling.
- **LO3:** Fair requirements elicitation; analysis and specification not sufficiently well described; clear picture is missing.
- **LO4:** Background research presented basically; some relevant material poorly presented or absent. Limited originality.
- **LO5:** Technically fair but not too challenging project. Minor development of existing and new skills. Has needed significant supervisor help.

- **LO2:** Insufficient project management; more time required to recover activities.
- **LO3:** Insufficient requirements elicitation; insufficient analysis and specification. Recoverable.
- **LO4:** Background research very weak or absent. Analysis presented incorrectly or confusingly. Recoverable.
- **LO5:** Insufficiently technical or challenging as presented but recoverable. Weak skill development. Significant supervisor help needed.
- **LO6:** Insufficient demonstration of understanding of legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability issues. Considered recoverable.
- **LO7:** Main design decisions weakly described. Testing very incomplete. Recoverable.
- **LO8:** Weakly written and partly incoherent report. Appraisal minimal or incorrectly presented. Conclusions non-informative and some are wrong.

### 20–29

LO1–LO9. A piece of work that fails to achieve the required standard. The project is significantly incomplete, closer to a submitted coursework.

- **LO1:** Significant omissions in demonstrating appropriate methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Poor project management; insufficient time to recover in remaining academic year.
- **LO3:** Significant omissions in requirements elicitation; significantly poor analysis and specification.

- **LO8:** Very poorly written and incoherent report. Most background material absent or contains factual errors. Most analysis incorrect or confusing. Conclusions non-informative and mostly wrong.
- **LO9:** Poor understanding demonstrated in viva; unable to explain parts. Software has considerably fewer features than adequate and is highly unstable.

### 1–19

LO1–LO9. A piece of work that significantly fails to achieve the required standard. The project is significantly incomplete. Effort is closer to a bad coursework.

- **LO1:** Significantly poor demonstration of appropriate methods, techniques and tools.
- **LO2:** Significantly poor project management; insufficient time to recover.
- **LO3:** Significant omissions in requirements elicitation; significantly poor analysis and specification.
- **LO4:** Background research of no real value. No critical analysis demonstrated.

- **LO6:** Significantly failed to mention any legal, social, ethical, professional and sustainability considerations.

- **LO8:** Very poorly written and incoherent report. Main themes not identified at all. Most background material absent or contains factual errors. No conclusions, or conclusions are wrong.

## How Will This Module Enhance Employability?

Students will be encouraged to explore the job market for potential jobs in their areas of interest. The student will propose a project that may be added to a portfolio they can demonstrate to a potential employer. The Final Project can be used by the student to convince a potential employer that they have the required skills and domain knowledge to undertake the work for a targeted job.

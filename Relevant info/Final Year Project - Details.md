Assignment Overview:

A successful Final Project is a combination of practical work and a report detailing the project development and a critical analysis of the resulting software/application/research output. The assessment comprises of four elements:

the Final Project Report (FPR),
the Final Project Code (FPC),  with the accessible link
the Final Project Video Demo (FVD) and
the Final Project Viva (FPV).
 

Assignment Objectives:

The main objectives of this assignment are to:

Demonstrate Practical Application: Develop and present a practical output, such as software, a working prototype, or research outcomes, that showcases the skills acquired during the course.
Produce a Comprehensive Written Report: Prepare a detailed report that documents the development process, including the scope, depth, and complexity of the project, as well as a critical analysis of the work.
Conduct Background Research: Perform thorough research to support the development of the project, demonstrating an understanding of existing knowledge and the application of appropriate techniques.
Develop New and Existing Skills: Enhance and apply technical, analytical, and research skills throughout the project, showcasing the ability to learn and innovate.
Apply Appropriate Methodologies: Select and implement suitable methodologies and techniques to carry out the project, ensuring a structured and logical approach to development.
Address Ethical, Legal, and Professional Issues: Identify and evaluate any relevant legal, social, ethical, professional, and sustainability issues associated with the project.
Critically Reflect and Evaluate: Reflect on the development process and critically evaluate the outcomes, identifying strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement.
Demonstrate Complexity and Originality: Produce a practical output that is complex, stable, robust, and original, demonstrating a high level of technical proficiency.
Prepare for Viva: Successfully prepare for and participate in a viva, demonstrating a deep understanding of the project, the development process, and the wider context of the work.
Receive and Apply Formative Feedback: Engage with formative feedback throughout the project lifecycle to refine and improve the quality of the work, ensuring continuous progress towards the final submission.
